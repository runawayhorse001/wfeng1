  4. Dual-Wind Discontinuous Galerkin Method

    5. The Dual-Wind Discontinuous Galerkin (DWDG) Method is one of the special Finite Element Method (FEM). The main difference from the standard FEM is that DWDG uses discontinuous discrete derivatives and the discontinuous discrete derivatives are defined from the trace operator(Fig.1). \mathbb{Q}_i^-(v)(x)=\left\{ \begin{array}{ll} \displaystyle\lim_{\substack{y\in K^-\\y\to x}} v(y)~~if ~~n_e^{(i)}<0,\\ \displaystyle\lim_{\substack{y\in K^+\\y\to x}} v(y)~~if ~~n_e^{(i)}\geq0, \end{array} \right., \mathbb{Q}_i^+(v)(x)=\left\{ \begin{array}{ll} \displaystyle\lim_{\substack{y\in K^+\\y\to x}} v(y)~~if ~~n_e^{(i)}<0,\\ \displaystyle\lim_{\substack{y\in K^-\\y\to x}} v(y)~~if ~~n_e^{(i)}\geq0. \end{array} \right.

    Related paper

    • Wenqiang Feng, Thomas Lewis, and Steven Wise. Discontinuous galerkin derivative operators with applications to second order elliptic problems and stability. Prepared, 2014.
    • Wenqiang Feng, Thomas Lewis. Dual-Wind Discontinuous Galerkin Method Implementation Details. Project report, 2014.
